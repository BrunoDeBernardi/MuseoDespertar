Recognized Authors

Antonio Berni

Antonio Berni was born in Rosario on May 14, 1905. After his first artistic training in his hometown, he took advantage of a series of scholarships to continue his apprenticeship in Europe. He returned to Argentina in the 1930s, a time when he discovered and was influenced by surrealism and Mexican muralism.
Later, his interest in the reality of the country made him rethink his course in painting: he moved away from the fantastic dimension and approached a more realistic painting, linked to a defense of the most dispossessed social classes. Along with Juan Carlos Castagnino, Enrique Policastro and Carlos Giambaggi founded New Realism, and collaborated with David Alfaro Siqueiros, the great Mexican muralist, on a mural in Natalio Botana's house.

Benito Quinquela Martín

Benito Quinquela Martín was an Argentine painter from the Buenos Aires neighborhood of La Boca. Quinquela Martín is considered the painter of ports and is one of the most popular painters in the country. His port paintings show the activity, vigor and rudeness of daily life in the port of La Boca. He had to work as a child carrying bags of coal and these experiences influenced the artistic vision of his works. He exhibited his works in several exhibitions held in the country and abroad, managed to sell several of his creations and donated many others.
